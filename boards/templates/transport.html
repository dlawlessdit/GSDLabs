Dublin Bus

  • {% blocktrans %} Step on the bus and give destination. Then give the exact amount in coins or use Leap Card. Press the ‘Stop’ button to stop the bus and get off {% endblocktrans %}
  • Buy a Leap Card online or at Payzone outlet (Spar, Centra, Gala etc.). You can top up online or at LUAS station. The minimum top up is 5£. It is useful on Dublin Bus, Dart, LUAS and Bus Eireann.
  • There are also sightseeing tours such as ‘hop-on hop-off’ Day Tours (24 Hour – 72 Hour tickets). Airlink Tickets to aiport. Dublin Bus 16 takes you to City Center from the Airport. There are also Ghostbus Tours at night.

  • Airport services, departs from Heuston Rail Station and Camden Charlotte Way. Total cost: 9.00£




DART


  • Book online or pay with cash at the station

  • Use Leap Card at tag-on point or buy ticket with cash at station terminals, then tag-off at similar point. Can only use Leap Card in Dublin region

  • May bring bicycle on Dart or park at station

  • Car parks available around Dart station, and disabled access such as disabled parking spaces and areas on Dart for wheelchair

  • May not bring strollers, personal luggage other than hand luggage or portable seating. You may bring small dogs and cats on board if on leash or on lap. Guide and assistance dogs only allowed in restaurant cars




LUAS


  • LUAS stands for Light Rail Transit System

  • Transport Infrastructure Ireland (TII) responsible for Luas

  • There are two Luas tram lines, Red Line and Green Line

  • Red Line 20kms in length with 32 Stops, runs from Tallaght to The Point, from Saggart to Connolly

  • Green Line 24.5km in length with 35 Stops, runs from Brides Glen to Broombridge

  • Designated crossing points at each stop, track level with pathway. Pedestrian crossings in city, cross roads and LUAS lines, use Safe Cross Code when crossing

  • Tram from Heuston to Abbey Street takes about 12 minutes, tram from Marlborough to Balally takes about 27 minutes. The pricing would be 5£ per trip.




Bus Eierann


  • Travels from Dublin to counties like Wexford, Wicklow, and Kildare

  • Does hop-on hop-off “Open-Road” ticket for freedom

  • Wild Atlantic Way, Donegal start, Cork end, goes travels along West Coast of Ireland

  • Ireland’s Ancient East, Dublin start and end via Newgrange Passage Tomb and Blarney Stone in Cork. The pricing would be 8£ per trip.
